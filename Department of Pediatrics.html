Department of Pediatrics

The Pediatrics Department receives all pediatric cases and diseases, examples of which are:


Gastrointestinal diseases
Endocrine diseases and diabetes
respiratory system diseases
Nervous system diseases
Immune system diseases
Pediatric rheumatology
Heart disease
Infectious diseases

And other diseases, in addition to complex and simple surgical cases such as open-heart surgeries

Kidney and urinary tract diseases in children

Metabolic diseases and methods of diagnosis and treatment

The children's department features:


Treatment of endocrine diseases in children under the supervision of specialized consultants

Providing care for pediatric diabetes under the supervision of a qualified staff that includes a pediatric diabetes consultant, a diabetes educator, a qualified nursing staff, and a nutritionist.

Growth hormone tests are performed by specialists

The presence of a vein detection device (Accuvein) to facilitate the process of installing intravenous needles for the child and so that the staff can provide distinguished nursing care to patients.

The department is divided into the following subunits:


Pediatric outpatient unit
Pediatric inpatient unit
Emergency unit
Neonatal unit

Intensive care unit for children from 6 months to 13 years

The neonatal intensive care unit provides the following services:


Infant clinic

It is a clinic specializing in the care of premature babies whose gestation period is thirty weeks or less. We are qualified to identify and deal with unstable heart and circulatory conditions, as well as digestive system problems, and care for newborns who have undergone surgical operations. The clinic also includes a group of staff with extensive experience in caring for children who suffer from physical deformities that are due to hereditary causes in the family (genetic deformities) or that occur during birth (congenital deformities). Ear, nose and throat

Treating conditions related to the ear, nose, and throat, such as ear infections, sinus problems, or breathing problems. Some treatments for these conditions may include ear tubes or tonsil surgery.


natural therapy

Special exercises to treat lung and breathing problems in newborns, such as those that may appear in premature babies.


Occupational therapy

Providing assessment services, sensory stimulation and movement exercises by a specialized therapist. This treatment is provided for special cases such as babies who are born prematurely and suffer from feeding problems. The Pediatric Department is also one of the main therapeutic departments that provides medical services to children aged between one day and 14 years in Alexandria. The department provides its services to all children, including pediatric emergency patients.


The Department of Pediatrics at City Hospital works in cooperation with other departments in the hospital, especially the Department of Pediatric Ear, Nose and Throat, the Department of Pediatric Orthopedics, the Department of Obstetrics and Gynecology, and the Department of Medical Laboratories in order to ensure that our patients receive the highest and most appropriate standards of care possible.
